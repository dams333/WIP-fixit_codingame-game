  The Goal

Fix the bugs and earn more money than your opponent.

  Rules

The game is played on a computer park consisting of 12x5 computers. You are in competition with another manager for the maintenance of the park.

Throughout the game, bugs will appear and evolve. Your goal will be to manage your Fixers team in order to earn more money than your opponent.
Beware, each Fixer will cost you money. You starts with 100 credits and must therefore avoid losing too many.

Fixers

Your Fixers are autonomous. All you have to do is tell them and they will automatically act on the computer to which they are assigned.
Each turn you can hire, fire or move as many Fixers as you like. Warning: There can only be one Fixer from each player per computer.
At the end of the turn, each Fixer causes the bug on the computer where it is present to lose 1 level.

Bugs

Regularly, bugs will appear on computers. These bugs can be level 1, 2 or 3. The credits will only be given to you when you make the bug disappear completely (when it goes from level 1 to level 0).
Bugs evolve regularly (never exceeding level 3). However, the credits you earn depends solely on the level of the bug at the time it appeared (yes, if you took too long to intervene, it's your fault, not the customer's).

Commands

Each turn you can use as many commands as you like, separated by ;
Possible commands are:
  • WAIT: Do nothing (If there are other commands, it will be ignored.)
  • HIRE <x> <y>: Hires a new Fixer at specified coordinates (Cost 10 credits)
  • MOVE <id> <x> <y>: Move specified Fixer to new coordinates
  • FIRE <id>: Fire the Fixer
Victory Conditions
  • After 200 turns, you have more credits than your opponent.
  • Your opponent can no longer pay his Fixers.
Defeat Conditions
  • You don't have enough money to pay your Fixers.
  • After 200 turns, you have less credits than your opponent.
  • Your program does not provide a valid command in time.


  Expert Rules

  • If you and your opponent have a Fixer on the same computer, the bug loses 2 levels. If the bug is solved, you both win the full amount.
  • Commands are applied in the order in which they are written. If a command cannot be executed (lack of money, no space on the computer, etc...), it is ignored and execution continues with the next command.
  • The credits you earn by fixing a bug are: initial_bug_level * 5.
  • Each Fixer costs you 1 credit if it is still in play at the end of the round.

  Instructions order for a turn

  • 1) Commands are applied
  • 2) Fixers still in play reduce bug level by 1
  • 3) You earn credits for solved bugs
  • 4) You pay 1 credit per Fixer still in play
  • 5) Bugs may evolve if enough time has elapsed
  • 6) A new bug may spawn if enough time has elapsed

  Game Input

Initialization input

Line 1: one integer mapWidth, the number of computers on the horizontal axis

Line 2: one integer mapHeight, the number of computers on the vertical axis

Input

Line 1: two integer myCredits and opponentCredits, the number of credits per player

Line 2: one integer spawnBug, the number of turns to wait before a new bug appears on the map. If the value is 0, a bug will appear at the end of the round.

Line 3: one integer caseCount, the number of computers

caseCount next lines: 3 integers to describe computers:

  • x: The computer position on the horizontal axis
  • y: The computer position on the vertical axis
  • bugLevel: The current bug level (0 if there is no bug)
  • upgradeBug: the number of turns to wait before the bug upgrade. If the value is 0, the bug will be upgraded at the end of the round (if it can be).

Next line: one integer fixerCount, the number of fixers

fixerCount next lines: 4 integers to describe Fixers:

  • id: The unique identifier of the Fixer
  • x: The Fixer position on the horizontal axis
  • y: The Fixer position on the vertical axis
  • owner:
    • 1: One of your Fixers
    • -1: One of your opponent Fixers
Output for One Game Turn

one line containing the commands, separated by ; Possible commands are:

  • WAIT: Do nothing
  • HIRE <x> <y>: Hires a new Fixer at specified coordinates
  • MOVE <id> <x> <y>: Move specified Fixer to new coordinates
  • FIRE <id>: Fire the Fixer
